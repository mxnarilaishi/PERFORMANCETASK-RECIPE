Sinigang shrimp

Shrimp Sinigang is a classic Filipino sour soup made with fresh shrimp simmered in a tangy broth flavored by tamarind, tomatoes, and onions. It’s often cooked with vegetables like kangkong (water spinach), radish, okra, and eggplant, creating a comforting balance of savory seafood and refreshing sourness. This dish is beloved for its hearty yet light taste, perfect with steamed rice and enjoyed during family meals or rainy days.


Bopis

Bopis is a traditional Filipino dish made from finely chopped pork lungs and heart, sautéed with garlic, onions, vinegar, and chilies. Known for its bold, tangy, and spicy flavor, it’s often enjoyed with rice or served as pulutan (beer food). Originating from Kapampangan cuisine, bopis is loved for its unique mix of savory organ meat and crunchy vegetables like carrots and bell peppers.


Tempura

Tempura is a popular Japanese dish made by coating seafood, vegetables, or sometimes meat in a light batter and deep-frying until crisp and golden. The batter, typically made from flour, egg, and cold water, creates a delicate crunch that highlights the natural flavor of the ingredients. Common versions include shrimp tempura, sweet potato, eggplant, and green beans. Tempura is often served with a dipping sauce called tentsuyu, made from soy sauce, mirin, and dashi, or simply with salt.
